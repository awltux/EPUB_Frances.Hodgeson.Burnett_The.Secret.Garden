* Martha's day off: Martha goes home to her mother, who is well-regarded by Mrs. Medlock.
* Mary's interest in the moor: Mary expresses a desire to see Martha's cottage, which is five miles across the moor.
* Martha's observations of Mary: Martha notices Mary's changing demeanor and curiosity.
* Ben Weatherstaff's knowledge: Ben Weatherstaff knows about the garden with the rose-trees, and that no one has been inside for ten years.
* Mary's growing fondness for people and places: Mary begins to like the garden, robin, Dickon, Martha's mother, and Martha.
* Discovery of the key: Mary finds an old key buried in the flowerbed near the ivy-covered wall, possibly the key to the secret garden.
* Robin's behavior: The robin seems to remember Mary and leads her to the key.
